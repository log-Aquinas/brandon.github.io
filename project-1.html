Adaptive Audio

What is adaptive audio and how to use it?

Video games give us a unique experience with sound because the environments events and behaviors they depict change according to a player's interaction.That means the video games contain adaptive audio (sounds and music that reacts and adapts to player input).Adaptive audio is deeply rooted in the nature of game audio ever since video game hardware could play sound samples, they've been using sounds to evoke places or actions when it's appropriate.

The most basic level of adaptive audio : getting a sound to play when something happens rather than at a predefined time. Let's imagine some games in development and apply this basic adaptive audio to them. In the game ,we have a character that can move around in environment by walking when the player inputs a direction. In order to evoke that sense of walking, we will add some footstep sounds. We could have the character's walking animation dictate when this footstep sound occurs right at any moment when their foot hits the ground.

Let's keep moving.We can use another game to explore more about adaptive audio.In the second game ,we have a race track and a rice car that plays a roaring engine sound while the player is pressing a button to accelerate.But part of this race track goes through a tunnel and while the engine sound evokes our car driving ,it doesn't differentiate between outdoors and in the tunnel.This is an interesting question, and we will solve it in the next part of the passage.

So, how can we make the sound different when we driving in the tunnel or outside?

The simplest way is to use different source of audio sample.But it's not the most smart way to do it.

Let's open FMOD Studio and drag the audio source on the track.

Distortion

Process audio by by increasing their gain, producing a "fuzzy", "growling", or "gritty" tone.

Autopitch

Change pitch of audio sample and simulate the sound we don't have.
